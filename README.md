# Turbine Blade Internal Cooling — CFD Study

A computational study of internal cooling performance in gas turbine blades
using serpentine channel configurations, simulated in ANSYS Fluent.

---

## Overview

This project investigates the heat transfer characteristics of serpentine
internal cooling channels in turbine blades. The analysis focuses on
understanding temperature distribution, coolant flow behaviour, and
thermal effectiveness across different channel configurations.

**Tools used:** ANSYS Fluent | ANSYS Meshing

---

## Key Results

- Temperature contours showing cooling effectiveness across blade cross-sections
- Comparison of flow behaviour at channel bends

> Result images available in `/results/`

---

## Methodology

1. Geometry setup — serpentine channel modelled within a normal axial turbine geometry in ANSYS BladeGen
2. Meshing — structured/unstructured mesh 
3. Solver setup — steady-state, pressure-based solver with SST k-w turbulence model
4. Post-processing — temperature contours extracted

---


## Notes

Full boundary conditions, mesh files, and solver settings are not included.
This repo is intended as a portfolio showcase of the simulation workflow and results.
